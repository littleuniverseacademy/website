Lunch, lunch break and guided activity time.

III. Afternoon programs (2:00PM - 5:00PM)

Chinese immersion program in the afternoon.

  • 2:00-3:00 Part-edition Chinese teaching (divided into classes according to level).
  • 3:00-4:00 Chinese culture class.
  • 4:00-5:00 Interesting Chinese characters and graded reading.

(In the camp, the teacher will swiftly arrange daily breaks with students)


Flexibility in camp schedule and combination: Our summer camp program offers ample resiliency in selection and designation. You can choose the morning program only or the afternoon program only; you can combine the morning with noon or noon with the afternoon; you can also choose the whole day. You can choose a one-week, two-week or three-week schedule. Since July and August programs have the same content, you can also align your attendance in any combination. For example, if you are only interested in learning Karate, you can choose the karate morning alone.

Teacher Profile

Karate is taught by Sensei Noah from Boston Budo. As for the detailed information, please go to: https://www.bostonbudo.com


Robotics is taught by Sam from ICRobot: https://www.icrobot.us


Music is taught by Siyu Pan. She is a mezzo-soprano who graduated from Nanjing Normal University with a bachelor's degree in music education, a double master's degree (vocal performance and music education) and a diploma in vocal performance from the Bard Longy Conservatory of Music in the United States. In 2019, she was invited to participate in the Cremona Music Festival in Italy and successfully participated in many vocal ensemble concerts. As a soloist, she has participated in many large-scale opera performances, created an impressive character image, and successfully held many solo concerts. She has participated in the American Classical Vocal Competition, the Hong Kong International Vocal Competition, the Hong Kong International Music Competition, etc., all of which have achieved excellent results. As a music educator, she has taught music courses (including choir, orchestra, percussion) at Boston Latin school and Orchard Gardens K-8 school, using different teaching modes and methods for students of all ages to make students study happily and efficiently.


Art is taught by Jianyi Gu. Ms. Gu graduated from the Academy of Fine Arts of Tsinghua University and she also has a degree from ESMOD·Beijing. she teaches at a top Academy of Fine Arts. "Art is not only a painting technique, but also the observation and perception of life, the experience and expression of life, and the collision of sensibility and logic." Therefore, Ms. Gu’s cultivating attitude is that thinking is more important than technology, and it is brought about by the need to express creativity. In the learning process, children should not pay too much attention to technology and imitation skills just to obtain picture effects, but should give enough space for children to express their ideas. In the picture effect, the original ecological effect of children's painting is also preserved as much as possible to protect their natural artistic sense and innocence.

My First Piano Adventure

Annie Chen, piano major in Berklee College of Music (with scholarship), a long-time piano educator.

The contents of the class includes tone training, piano improvisation, listening and singing, rhythm training, classical music appreciation, music games, and short stories of music history. In the course, students are trained to use a high degree of concentration, judgment, memory and instant reaction ability to feel music, accept and express music.

In the stage of rapid growth of children's hearing ability, music courses of listening, feeling and performance are introduced, focusing on cultivating children's sense of sound and hearing. Children experience the emotions and feelings brought by music, and try to express them in the way of singing and playing, and use musical scores to confirm the sounds played and sung, so that children can become small musicians that plays and performs freely in future. For young children, music interactive games can improve their attention, concentration, hands and feet integration ability, group interaction and clear expression ability. Through the process of playing, they can gradually learn to evaluate their own skills and improve their personal ability.

Mandarin for beginners

Ms. Guan has more than 10 years of Chinese teaching experience. Prior to teaching in Boston, she worked as a Chinese teacher for 4 years at the prestigious training institution Cheongdam Language School in Seoul, South Korea. In Boston, she worked as a high school Chinese teacher at Milton Academy, known as the "Feeder School (direct school)" of Harvard University. Ms. Guan is adept at communicating with children and creates a relaxed and entertaining environment to promote her students' curiosity in Chinese.


How will the class be taught?


  • Classroom themes are closely related to student's personal lives and immediate surroundings.
  • Learning goals crafted to suit student' developmental progress at each grade level
  • Essential vocabulary and grammatical structures tailored for each grade level
  • Students will be exposed to five learning aspects: Listening, speaking, reading, writing, and cultural competency
  • Mandarin classes will be taught using simplified character and Pinyin

Themes and Units

Semester One - All about me

  • My Introduction (Who am I?)
  • My Information (What do you need to know about me?
  • My Family (What do you need to know about me from just looking at me?
  • My Heritage (What you don't know about me from just looking at me?
  • My Food & Drinks Preferences (What do I like and don't like to eat and drink?
  • My Interest & Hobbies (What do I like to do on my free time? )
  • My Body & Health (How does my body feel and react? )
  • My Feelings and Emotions (How does my mind feel, and how does my body react? )

Semester Two - All around me

  • My Home (Things I see or do inside my home )
  • My Home (Things I see or do outside and around my home )
  • My School (What and who do I see in my class and school? )
  • My School (What do I do in my class and school? )
  • My Community (Different places that make up my community )
  • My Community (People who work in our community)
  • My Trips (Where in our community or in the world can we go?)

French for beginners

Audrey Wilson is a Franco-American teacher, originally from Paris, France. My passion is teaching kids about her multicultural world and providing an immersive experience with creative and fun activities. She has 5 years of tutoring and teaching experience with kids aged 5 to 16 years old, with all levels of French.


The beginner course will guide the students through learning the language, geography, and cultures of France. The first year is designed for students to learn to communicate in everyday situations through dialogues, games, projects, and group activities.


By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Say the alphabet and count in French.
  2. Engage in simple conversations in French.
  3. Describe themselves and others in French.
  4. Talk about their family and home in French.
  5. Listen and learn about French music.
  6. Understand French history and cultural traditions.
  7. Make connections between the French language and roots of the English language.
  8. Compare and contrast French culture and American culture (for example).

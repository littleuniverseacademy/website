Summer Camp, 2025

I. Specific Arrangements

The 2025 Summer Camp lasts for six weeks, and each week features different content. Classes are held daily from 9am to 6pm (please refer to the schedule —the end of this page—for details). No need to worry about the learning level, as the teachers are very experienced and will adjust the schedule according to the child's age and learning progress.


Flexible Program Selection: Our summer camp offers a wide range of program options. You can choose to attend morning classes only, afternoon classes only, morning and noon programs, noon and afternoon programs, or full-day options. You can sign up for one week or multiple weeks.

II. Special Notes

Please bring your own lunch. The school provides a refrigerator and microwave.


Teachers will allocate rest time reasonably based on student needs.


Karate classes include a belt, and the uniform set is priced at $45. You can choose to purchase it, bring your own, or wear regular clothes. Please pay the uniform fee directly to Sensei Noah.


Art courses cover multiple art disciplines, and the cost of all materials is included in the tuition, with no extra charge.


The Singapore Math program will provide a relavant workbook based on the child's learning level.


Our partners include IC Robot, Boston Budo, StyleMe, and so on. Additionally, chess will be managed by Chess Master Tamer Karatekin (FM, FT, FSI), in partnership with the Boston Children’s Chess Foundation and Harvard Square Chess Community.


All teachers in the camp are experienced professionals loved by the children


Each student will receive a T-shirt (at the beginning of the camp) and a beautifully crafted album (created after the camp).

IV. Teacher Profiles

Introduction of some teachers, for more information, please visit above websites

1) Director: Yang Feng

Master of Human Development and Psychology from Harvard Graduate School of Education; Master of American Literature.

Ms. Feng is a certified teacher in both China and the United States. In recent years, she has been primarily engaged in education, and is familiar with teaching philosophies such as Reggio-inspired Style, Montessori, and Free Play Style, with extensive teaching experience.

Ms. Feng is the founder of Little Universe Academy (LUA). From China to the United States, Ms. Feng has been dedicated to the field of education, and teaching and nurturing children is both her passion and mission.

2) Karate: Noah Lucia

Noah began his formal Uechi Ryu training when he was 3 years old. He grew up in his father's dojo but split time during his early life between karate, judo and ice hockey, which he played at Norwich University. Shortly after graduating in 2008, he traveled to Japan where he taught English and studied Japanese Kempo. With this advanced and diverse experience, combined with his father's teaching, Noah continues to develop the dojo’s unique style. He’s also continuing the family’s tradition as his two toddler daughters already take classes from their grandfather (Papa Sensei).

3) Chess: Tamer Karatekin

Tamer (born in 1981 in Yugoslavia) is a dual-career chess master, a renowned children's chess coach, and a prolific scholastic chess entrepreneur. He is a former Turkish Chess Champion in 2000 and a former first board player on MIT’s chess team. He is most well known for his draws against world chess champions Kasparov and Karpov in simultaneous chess shows. He has coached numerous medalists in the World and European Youth and School chess championships. Tamer is also the designer of the SAHI chess set and chess icons and the curator of the SHATRANJ.ART exhibition on historic mind games and chess sets.

In addition to his chess-related endeavors, Tamer is a computer scientist, grant writer, project coordinator, technology product manager, and artificial intelligence educator. He leads the EU-funded SHATRANJ.AI project to build an artificial intelligence curriculum based on shatranj and other historic mind and board games. His company, Deep Sea Chess, maintains a free-access and culturally inclusive early childhood chess curriculum.

4) Chorus: Xuanhao Li

Ms. Li graduated from Longy School of Music. She is a Master of Music, young soprano, and she formerly worked at the Central Conservatory of Music Choir.

Educational Background:

Starting from 2014, studied under Professor Chao Haojian of the PLA Academy of Art and renowned tenor Dai Yuqiang.

In 2016, pursued further studies at the Tchaikovsky Conservatory in Russia and participated in numerous international vocal masterclasses and workshops. Received guidance from renowned mentors including Professor Max Bro, a pianist from La Scala Opera House in Italy, mezzo-soprano Jiaowana, Professor Cristina Rubin from Milan Conservatory, and renowned baritone Paolo Coni. Learned authentic Italian bel canto vocal technique.

Awards and Performance Experience:

In 2016, performed at the Tchaikovsky Conservatory's 150th Anniversary Concert in Russia, singing an aria from the opera "Tosca" and won the Gold Prize at the Tchaikovsky International Vocal Competition.

In 2016, participated in the highC China National Soprano Competition, winning the Gold Prize in the preliminary round in Jilin Province, the Gold Prize in the semi-final round in Beijing, and the Gold Prize in the national final.

In 2016, invited to give a demonstration lecture for music teachers in Suzhou City.

In 2017, received the Third Prize in the Renato Bruson International Vocal Competition.

Released her debut single "King Dragon Glorious" in December.

Participated in various performances and concerts including orchestras, opera productions, and solo recitals.

In 2022, she was accepted with outstanding performance into the Longy School of Music in the United States, pursuing a Master of Music degree. Participated in major projects such as Mass Opera and La Voce Totale during her studies.

Ms. Li has over ten years of vocal teaching experience, covering students of all ages from children to adults, exam-oriented students to seniors. Her teaching methods are flexible and tailored to each student's singing issues. Utilizing her superb vocal technique and years of teaching experience, she offers students guidance in pitch, rhythm, tone, resonance, vocal color, articulation, emotion, breath control, and more. Teacher Li possesses ample stage performance experience and excellent piano accompaniment skills, delivering teaching of the highest professional standard. She aims to enhance students' vocal qualities comprehensively, arranging ABRSM vocal exams and recitals accordingly. Her goal and expectation are to help students appreciate the beauty of music, enjoy and love music, and open doors to a better life through music.

5) Arts: Peixian Yao

Ms. Yao graduated from Accademia Di Belle Arti di Cuneo in Italy, where she completed both her undergraduate and master’s degrees in the Painting Department. After graduation, she has worked as an art teacher in an art studio in Beijing, teaching students ranging in age from 5 to 60, with 4 years of teaching experience. In addition to regular art courses, she also coaches students for art exams and art competitions
